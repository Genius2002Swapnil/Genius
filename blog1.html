How to Start Your Law Journey in India – A Beginner’s Guide

By Genius Era is Back

🎯 Why Choose Law?

Law is not just about arguing in court. It's a powerful tool to bring justice, shape society, and even govern the nation. From legal activism to UPSC and LLM abroad – law opens many doors.

📚 3-Year vs 5-Year Law Course

  • 5-Year LLB: After 12th (e.g., BA LLB, BBA LLB). Offered by NLUs and top private colleges.
  • 3-Year LLB: After graduation. Offered by DU, BHU, GLC Mumbai etc.

📝 Entrance Exams You Should Know

  • CLAT: Common Law Admission Test for NLUs (UG + PG)
  • AILET: For NLU Delhi
  • CUET: For central universities like DU
  • LSAT: For private colleges like Jindal

🏫 Top Law Colleges in India

  1. NLSIU Bengaluru
  2. NLU Delhi
  3. WBNUJS Kolkata
  4. GNLU Gandhinagar
  5. Symbiosis, Pune
  6. Faculty of Law, DU

📖 What Will You Study?

Core subjects include: Constitutional Law, IPC/BNS, CrPC/BNSS, Evidence Act, Contract, Torts, Family Law, Jurisprudence etc.

⚖ Internships & Moot Courts

Law school is incomplete without real-world experience. Intern under lawyers, judges, law firms, NGOs. Participate in moot courts, debates and legal aid camps.

🚀 Career Paths After Law

  • Judiciary (Civil Judge, Magistrate)
  • UPSC/IAS
  • Litigation or Law Firms
  • LLM in India or Abroad
  • Legal Journalism, Policy, Academia

💡 Final Tips from SKR

🔹 Start early with current affairs and basic legal knowledge.
🔹 Build a strong resume with internships and writing.
🔹 Be patient – law is a marathon, not a sprint.
